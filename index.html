What We Do

Scientific Clarity, Applied to Assets and Operations.

Based in the West Kootenay region of British Columbia, Climatic Resources Consulting (CRC) is a data-driven, climate resilience software and consulting company. We provide leading edge climate projections and quantitative risk analysis that facilitate the prioritisation of climate risks within asset management structures.

The approach and related software, Localised Climate Analysis and Projections (LCap), uses statistical downscaling and probabilistic analysis to generate site-specific climate projections. LCap enables planners and engineers to use a probability-based approach to undertake targeted climate change planning for specific community operations and infrastructure.

Quantitative Risk Analysis

Threshold exceedance probabilities for climate-sensitive infrastructure and services.

Asset Management Integration

Decision-ready climate data integrated into resilience and capital planning workflows. Quantitative prioritization of adaptation actions for assets and operations.

Case Studies

Probability in Practice.

City of Edmonton Collaboration

CRC developed and applied the LCap framework to support probability-based climate planning for City of Edmonton operations and infrastructure. The work combined a local climate history with downscaled projections and translated those results into exceedance probabilities for planning-relevant thresholds. This allowed technical teams to compare risk trajectories across multiple time horizons and prioritize adaptation actions with stronger quantitative support.

Building Cooling Cost Thresholds

This case study assessed future cooling cost exposure by estimating the probability of exceeding specific budget thresholds under projected warming conditions. Rather than relying on average change alone, the analysis focused on likelihood and frequency of cost stress events that matter to facilities management. The results supported more resilient budgeting, lifecycle planning, and phased investment decisions for building operations.

Nordic Ski Area Viability

For a Nordic ski operation in British Columbia, CRC produced a 100-year climate history and 21st-century projection set, then linked those datasets to operational snow and temperature thresholds. LCap-based probability outputs helped clarify how reliability windows may shift over coming decades.
