Skillset Summary

  • Proficiency in compiling regional and local climate model information and analyzing historical climate timeseries with appropriate statistical techniques.
  • Research experience developing novel approaches for quantifying probability of climate thresholds being exceeded for infrastructure and operations.
  • Strong communication skills for translating complex scientific concepts to technical and non-technical audiences.
  • Project leadership across multidisciplinary research, academic programs, workshops, and applied climate initiatives.
  • Extensive writing and review experience in research papers, books, grant proposals, newsletters, and media-facing material.

Professional and Academic Experience

Selected Contributions

2017 - 2023 | Climatic Resources Consulting

Contributed to major climate change assessment work for the City of Edmonton, reconstructing local climate history and projecting future conditions using an ensemble of downscaled models. This work included annual and seasonal projections plus a comprehensive set of climate indices, and supported probability-based planning for infrastructure and operations.

2007 - 2020 | Columbia Basin Trust Climate Action Programs

Supported regional climate action programming in partnership with local governments, First Nations, provincial agencies, and community stakeholders. Work focused on practical pathways for reducing greenhouse gas emissions and preparing for climate change impacts across the Columbia Basin.
